Aggro

  • Monsters will attack you if they are aggro'ed on you. Aggro is a state that is set when a monster is disturbed, either by a player, a pet, an NPC. A monster with a higher level than you has a chance that it will attack you if you are within a certain distance of it.
  • Monsters aggro'ed on you are indicated by an arrow as shown. Monsters aggro'ed on you try to attack you until you are dead, they are dead, you are 24 meters or more away, or another party member generated more aggro than you.
  • Bosses don't follow the 24 m rule, and will pursue you until one of you dies. Minibosses will stop attacking if you go a considerable distance away from where they spawned.
  • All aggro is reset if you change the map. Learn more about aggro mechanics at the Aggro section.

Combat is not Automated!

Toram Online tutorial says that Combat is automated. This is only partially true. Pressing the action button once selects the nearest mob and switches the camera to it. Pressing it again makes your character continuously Normal attack it until it dies, after that, your character sheathes your weapon.

The normal attacks are the only things automated in combat. You cannot automatically switch to a different mob. You either press the action button to select the nearest mob or if you want to fight a specific faraway mob, click on that mob. You can press TAB to see a list of all mobs and players on the map.

Normal attacks deal pathetically low amounts of damage once you reach level 50 or above. (Even at level 25-30 they are weak). The main damage dealing concept in Toram Online are Skills , specifically attacking skills. Those are NOT AUTOMATED and require you to focus to use skills at the right time in the right order or to know when to stop using skills and dodge.

Therefore, COMBAT IN TORAM ONLINE IS NOT AUTOMATED AND REQUIRES A LOT OF WORK . If you favor automated combat, Toram is not for you.

Channels, Servers and Worlds

Toram Online is a multiplayer game. Unlike Albion Online, the game does not run on a single server where each logged in player can see every other logged in player. In that sense, Toram online is not open world sandbox.

Server

A server is a computer program or device that provides a service to another computer program and its user, also known as the client. Every player connects to a server located at Asobimo Inc. (Toram Online). The server is responsible for managing the game and the players. Learn more about servers at Wikipedia.

We are not sure how Asobimo Inc. hosts their servers, whether there are separate ones for International, Japan, Global, English, etc.

World

A server is often sharded within itself to reduce load on its computing power. A world is a shard of a server. (It means that the physical server is divided into multiple worlds, each with its own computing)

These are the worlds in the game :

Android/iOS

  • Global World 1
  • International World 1
  • International World 2
  • International World 3
  • Regional Language World 1

Desktop

  • Global World 2
  • International World 4

Depending on the availability of players in the world, the world may lag. However, reports of lag due to server overload are rare. If the world capacity if full, then you cannot join the world. This is true if you logged out and then the world becomes full. You cannot rejoin the world again if it filled up while you were offline. This information is important because filled servers are much more likely to have a greater number of players for trading and playing with.

Map

A map is place where your player can move in. The maps are not continuous, hence monsters cannot follow you from one map to another. The map is also not always accurately depicted by the minimap, since different levels of elevation are not always shown.

All the areas you can physically see cannot always be accessed. Sometimes, unconventional routes are used to access the areas. Some of them lead to secret areas. Try to find any if you can!

Using the ⟬ TAB ⟭ key on Desktop and holding the ⟦ Action ⟧ key on Mobile, you can see what areas are accessible from your current location, alongside players and monsters.

Channel

Even if two players are in the same map, they cannot see each other's characters or fight the same monster together if they are not in the same channel. A channel is a way of reducing rendering overhead. It is a way of grouping players together. A channel is a sub-dimension of the map.

Channels are created depending upon the population of the map. A channel is created automatically when the population of the map is greater than a certain threshold. You cannot enter a channel if it is full.

You can consider a channel to be an alternate reality version of a map. Changing channels is akin to moving in the 5th dimension.

And lastly, random players cannot (or should not be able to) attack the same monster together, unless they are in the same party (or if in a raid, in the same alliance/guild). Sometimes, you might start attacking some other party's monster, but that is a bug. This is a known issue.

Ease of Playing

  • There are a lot of things not mentioned in Toram's tutorial that makes your experience of Toram much much easier. Here are some of them.
  • Remember when I said that selecting a mob causes the camera to lock onto it? If you don't like this, you can change it using
    「Menu」➡「Settings」➡「System」➡「Autofocus」➡「Off」.
  • If you want to run faster, you can go to
    「Menu」➡「Settings」➡「System」➡「Dash」➡「On」.
  • If you don't like the annoying numbers that always pop up when you deal damage, go to
    「Menu」➡「Settings」➡「Graphics」➡「Damage Font Size」➡「(change slider)」.
  • If you don't like the annoying spammers spamming in the 'SHOUT' section of the chat
    「Menu」➡「Settings」➡「Chat」➡「Chat Filtering Settings」➡「Toggle Shout」.
  • If your phone can't handle Graphics, go to
    「Menu」➡「Settings」➡「Graphics」and check out the settings.
  • You can fiddle around with a lot of settings here, it would be really useful.
  • MENU -> Map -> World map are also helpful. The update in June 2021 has made it even easier to search and teleport to places.
  • For Making the red AOE clearer go to 「Menu」➡「Settings」➡「Graphics」. Scroll down to bottom and find 「Atk Warning Quality」and「Display Settings」.

Shortcuts

  • In「Menu」➡「Settings」➡「System」➡「Shortcuts」, the different sets are literally different sets of shortcuts, for different situations. You cannot access a shortcut from set 2 if set 1 is enabled.
    • The list is also a shortcut menu. You can tap the top of the page where it says「Page 1 ⬅」 and go to next page. Then tap the bottom "Shortcuts Off" to turn them on.
    • How to access this list? On the home screen, there is a literal button called 'LIST'. Tap the top to go to next page, tap the bottom to close list.
  • Tap on your name when on the Home screen. You can set up Auto Potion (you need to have potions in your inventory and potions are not unlimited). You can also set up at what % of HP you want to consume the potion.
  • Go to 「Menu」➡「Settings」➡「Guard/Evasion settings」➡「Set both to Manual A」. Those will be easier for you now. Once you get more understanding of those, you can change them later.

Inventory

  • You access your inventory by 「Menu」➡「Items」. Unlike most games, equipping a weapon/armor doesn't vanish it from the inventory.
  • There are 50 inventory spaces, each can hold 99 items. A group of 99 items is called a stack.
    • Weapons and armor and additional gear and ring are non-stackable.
  • You have a storage space, similar to a 'bank locker'. You can access it at any NPC store that has that feature. Usually in Sofya City, El Scaro, and Hora Diomedea. (Unfamiliar names, yes, but soon they will be familiar.)
    • This also has storage space. For increasing storage space in bank you can use orbs.
    • One orb increases one storage space in personal inventory. (which you can move around)
    • One orb opens one inventory slot, but one orb opens 5 bank slots. Choose carefully.
  • When in your inventory, tap an item. On the left are options on what to do with that item. Tap the「1/2」 icon to open up more options on what to do with that item.

Stats

Early game, monsters are fairly easy, so it is recommended to add all stat points to boost ATK/MATK. For now, you don't have to worry about VIT stat. Defense, MaxHp, and MaxMP can be gained from equipments.

HIT and FLEE are useless, as of given date above. The game is very unbalanced with respect to those. Monsters have unreasonably high HIT and FLEE compared to players. Don't try to increase these.

'But how can I not miss if I don't increase Accuracy?'
  • Magic never misses, but can be guarded. It also cannot be evaded.
  • Criticals never miss either, but their damage fluctuates a lot if they graze. (If an attack was going to miss but it was also critical, it will graze. Graze reduces stability.)

Levels

  • The current level cap is 230 as of the given above date.
  • Toram Online is a 2015 game, so obviously, most players are high level and most events are aimed at level 150+ players. But, there are also events for all levels. You need not worry.
  • Money, (currency called spina) is currently earned by mass killing mobs with an AOE and selling the materials (mats) to the NPC stores. The good AOE skills unlock at level 70, and if you need boosts on those you need to be level 150+
  • If you don't want money and want to enjoy the game and the story normally while defeating mobs with hard work, you can do that. However, don't expect us to help you. That is because we already have a helping system in place by the name of mercenaries.
  • Using「MENU」➡「Community」➡「Guild」➡「Mercinaries」or just simply friend mercenaries or even hired mercenaries, you can easily fight bosses. Furthermore, if a boss is hard, you need to exit (standing on exit for 10 seconds) and reenter in easy mode.
  • That's why, if you chose to do story mode while having fun, (while I will help because I enjoy helping) players will not help you and suggest you to put down your pride and do it in easy mode. If you want to do story with hard work and the feeling of winning, you need to do it on your own (or get a new player like you alongside)
  • Iv'e already told you why people want to level up to level 150, it's to get stronger to fight mobs to earn spina.

Skills

  • Toram has a classless system meaning you can get skills from any skill tree. The weapon you choose in Tutorial only determines which skill page you unlock first.
  • To upgrade skills you need to get skill pages. Those can be bought at the Sofya City library. Some pages are in books that you can't find in the library. Those books have special skills. Those can be purchased from other players or be won as prizes from treasure chests. (Complete chapter 2 lesson 1 to understand what those are.)'
  • Skill pages are divide into tiers. Tier 0 to 4.
    • Most (NOT ALL) main weapon skill pages from tier 0 and 1 are unlocked at level 0
    • Tier 2 pages unlock at level 30
    • Tier 3 pages unlock at level 70
    • Tier 4 pages unlock at level 150
  • Those levels are the levels of your highest leveled character. If you have a level 120 character and a level 40 character, then even the level 40 character can use tier 3 skills. If you then delete the level 120 character, then whatever pages you had purchased for the level 40 character remain. But you cannot get new pages.
  • Tier 4 skills are the strongest and help you level your other characters faster and also farm faster. Hence everyone keeps telling you to do story up to chapter 9 and to get to level 150. If you want, you can enjoy the game at your own pace (It helps you learn combat skills and mechanics. I recommend it) But you won't find many people around your level because, Toram is an old game. People are already high level.

Trading and Economy

  • You can trade with other people by tapping on them and clicking trading. You don't need to actually give them anything in trade, or even coins, though. You need to be able to see them though.
    • As always, more options show up on list when you tap the top arrow.
  • You can gift a friend, party member or a guild member from long distance. This is however only one item at a time. You can also mail them over long distances, so they can see it when they come back online.
  • Sofya City, El Scaro and Hora Diomedea have Trading boards called consignment boards. You can register your item there, and people will search for your item and buy it. However, the government taxes you. You can even do cross - server in this method, but then there's an ADDITIONAL tax.

Orbs

  • If you want to spend money on this game, you can spend your orbs however you want to. Toram is not P2W anyway. There is nothing to 'win' either.
